Walk-The-Dog Technique

Classic WTD — Rod tip pointed DOWN at 45°. Short downward twitches while slowly reeling. Each twitch makes the lure dart left-right. Rhythm is everything: twitch...twitch...twitch.

Pro Tip — Let the lure sit completely still for 3–5 seconds after a cast near structure. Jacks and roosters can't resist it.

Proven Shore Tactics
01

Fan Cast the Beach — Cover Water First

Don't stay in one spot. Cast at 10 o'clock, 11 o'clock, and 12 o'clock positions to fan the water in front of you. If no strikes in 6–8 casts, move 30 yards down the beach and repeat. Jacks and ladyfish roam — you need to find them.

KastmasterCrystal Minnow
02

Cast Parallel to Shore Along Sandbars

The trough between the beach and the outer sandbar is prime ambush territory. Cast parallel to the beach (not straight out) and retrieve along the edge of the bar. Predators patrol this lane constantly looking for trapped baitfish.

KastmasterBucktail JigSwimbait
03

Match the Topwater Window Precisely

The Super Spook is a finesse play for calm, low-light conditions only. Don't use it mid-day in choppy surf — it will be ignored. Save it specifically for the first and last 90 minutes of light. When conditions are right, it can be the deadliest lure in your bag for roosterfish.

Super Spook
04

Watch for Birds — Then Get There Fast

Frigate birds and pelicans diving means actively feeding fish below. When you see this, immediately switch to the fastest lure you have (Kastmaster) and power-cast into the middle of the action. You have 3–5 minutes before the school disperses. Don't waste time changing leaders.

KastmasterCrystal Minnow
05

Slow Down When Fish Follow But Don't Strike

If you see a fish following your lure to the beach without committing, your retrieve is too fast. Drop to the swimbait or bucktail jig and slow everything down. Give the fish an easier decision — pause the lure, let it flutter, and often the strike comes on the fall.

SwimbaitBucktail Jig
06

Roosterfish: Long Pauses Win

If roosterfish are your target, they respond to patience. Cast the Super Spook or Crystal Minnow near the resort's dock pilings or any structure, twitch twice, then hold completely still for 5–8 seconds. Roosters will often circle and strike during the pause. Resist the urge to keep moving the lure.
